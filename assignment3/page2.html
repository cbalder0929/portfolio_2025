Tacos de Birria

Guajillo base stew with goat meat cooked overnight

Birria tacos trace their origins to the Mexican state of Jalisco, where birria—a slow-cooked, spiced stew made traditionally with goat meat—was first created. Rooted in the cultural traditions of celebration and communal meals, birria became a beloved dish for its bold flavors and tender texture. Over time, chefs transformed it into tacos by stuffing the flavorful meat into tortillas, often served with a side of rich consommé for dipping. Today, birria tacos have gained global popularity, celebrated for their savory, spicy, and deeply satisfying taste.
